10. ROOT CAUSE HYPOTHESIS
================================================================================

The robot fails not because the trot pattern is broken, but because:

UNDERPOWERED STANCE LEGS
  During each swing phase, the two stance legs must support 100% of body weight.
  The MPC controller cannot generate sufficient vertical force from these legs.
  
Symptoms:
  1. Body sinks progressively (pz drops 3.3cm over 4.5s)
  2. Weight ratio spikes during transitions (>1.2)
  3. GRF varies significantly (147-191N) indicating unstable control
  4. Pitch grows as controller compensates
  5. Failure occurs precisely at swing transitions (t=5.39s)

Contributing Factors:
  1. Low leg spring stiffness (if using springs)
  2. Low PD gains in swing leg controller
  3. Insufficient feed-forward forces in MPC
  4. Ground compliance too stiff (harsh impacts)
  5. Timing mismatch (legs hitting ground before ready)
  6. Contact model not accounting for impact damping
